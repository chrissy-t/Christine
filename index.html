The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People

by Stephen R. Covey

In the 1989 self help book, The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People, Covey talks about basic, primary habits that are the fundamental principles for human effectiveness. He defines a habit as The intersection of knowledge, skill and desire. Covey explains that we must allow ourselves to undergo paradigm shifts so that we can change ourselves and how we see the world.

The first three habits in the book deal with self awareness and moving from a state of dependence to independence.

  • Habit one: Be Proactive
    Covey discusses that we should use our self awareness to be proactive and take responsibility for our decisions and behaviour. We should not blame the world for our problems or let outside influences dictate our actions. Proactive people focus on things that they can actually change and are, therefore, more effective people. Our ability to chose and be the creative force of our own life seperates us from animals and we should not go through life as a reactive person
  • Habit two: Begin with the end in mind
    Covey tells us that we should keep our destination in mind so that each day of our life contributes in a meaningful way to the vision of your life as a whole. Only when we begin with the end in mind we can start to be truley effective in not only our own lives but in a business perspective as well. This habit is based on principles of personal leadership. Being a good leader is about doing the right things and setting a clear vision for the organization. Leadership comes before management.
  • Habit three:Put things first
    This habbit is The personal fruit, the practical fulfillment of Habits 1 and 2. Covey tells us that in order to manage ourselves effectively we must have the discipline to prioratize and put things first. All activities can be categorized based on what is important and not important. We need to reflect on these activities and schedule our priorities while having the willpower to do something when we may not want to do it.Covey advises us that we need to act according to our values rather than our desires.

A quote from The 7 Habbits of Highly Effective People that I found helpful:

It's incredibly easy to get caught up in an activity trap, in the busyness of life, to work harder and harder at climbing the ladder of success only to discover it's leaning against the wrong wall.

To me, this quote shows how people would often find themselves working or achieving victories that are not fullfuling. Sometimes we get so fixated on mundane things that are not truley valuable to us and and our drive to achieve our goal blinds us from the things that actually matter. As Covey stated in the book, when we have a clear understanding of what is important to us and create our own definition of success, we can have more efficent and fullfuling lives.
